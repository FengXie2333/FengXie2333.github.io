Neoantigen-directed immune escape in lung cancer evolution

Posted on 2019-03-30 | In paper

Neoantigen-directed immune escape in lung cancer evolution

Topic

To determine immune infiltration in untreated non-small-cell lung cancer (NSCLC), assess how this infiltration varies between and within tumours, and characterize immune-evasion mechanisms and their associations with clinical outcome.

Sample

Integrated 164 RNA-sequencing (RNA-seq) samples from 64 tumours and 234 tumour-infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL) histopathology estimates (hereafter, pathology TIL estimates) from 83 tumours to produce a combined cohort of 258 tumour regions from 88 prospectively acquired tumours within the ‘Tracking Non-Small-Cell Lung Cancer Evolution through Therapy’(TRACERx) 100 cohort

Results

Heterogeneity of immune infiltration

benchmarked published in silico immune deconvolution tools

The genes used in each one of the immune-estimation approaches were tested to see whether they fit two criteria: (1) having a negative relationship with tumour purity, as genes that define immune subtypes are expressed in infiltrating immune cells and (2) not showing a positive correlation with tumour copy number at the gene locus (a positive correlation may indicate that the gene is expressed by the tumour cell, which would confound immune estimates)

for each method, the immune estimates themselves were compared against independent ground-truth measures

 

conclusion: Compared to other transcriptomic approaches, the Danaher et al. immune signature optimally estimated immune infiltrates in NSCLC
