The biology and function of fibroblasts in cancer

发表于 2019-04-13 | 更新于 2019-04-27 | 分类于 paper

Abstract

Fibroblasts are usually quiescent and become activated in a wound healing response. The governing principles of how quiescent, resting fibroblasts become ‘activated’ is still being unravelled, and exciting new information suggests that there might be two types of fibroblast activation profile: ‘reversible’ and ‘irreversible’, determined partly by epigenetic regulation. Activated fibroblasts were identified predominantly by their expression of α-smooth muscle actin (αSMA; also known as ACTA2)

(Fibroblast 一般是静默状态，在组织损伤时会被激活。activated fibroblast又可分为可逆的活化和不可逆的活化)

Resting fibroblasts may share many features with MSC precursors.

MSC(Mesenchymal stem cells 间充质干细胞)

Quiescent versus activated fibroblasts

Fibroblasts and mesenchymal stem cells

it is well established that embryonic and umbilical cord‑derived fibroblasts and MSCs exhibit enhanced survival and stem cell‑like properties compared with those isolated from adult tissue. The collective evidence suggests that quiescent or resting fibroblasts are precursors of activated fibroblasts, which could also be called MSCs. Whether all activated fibroblasts are MSCs or only a subset that exhibits such properties remains to be determined and could depend on whether identical culture conditions are used

(activated fibroblast 也可称为MSC)

Wound healing, fibrosis and cancer

Acute wound healing

Once the wound is repaired, the number of activated fibroblasts decreases significantly owing to apoptosis, and the resting phenotype is probably restored. Such reversibility is the hallmark feature of tissue repair associated with wound healing.

Cancer fibrosis

  • Tumours are considered ‘wounds that do not heal’. Persistent emergence and accumulation of cancer cells in a given tissue represents an ongoing tissue injury, initiating a chronic wound healing response towards the cancer cells.(肿瘤可以被当成无法修复的组织损伤)
  • The term tumour fibrosis is interchangeable with desmoplastic reaction, tumour stroma and TME（结缔组织增生，肿瘤基质，TME）
  • Although the role of myofibroblasts in wound healing is well understood, their functional role in can- cer progression and metastasis is emerging as being complex and bimodal, with both cancer- promoting and cancer-restraining actions
  • The recruitment of stromal fibroblasts to the tumour is largely governed by the growth factors released by the cancer cells and the infiltrating immune cells. TGFβ, PDGF and fibroblast growth factor 2 (FGF2) are key mediators of fibroblast activation in acute and chronic tissue damage. The recruitment of activated fibroblasts in many cancers is dependent on TGFβ
  • Many studies have shown that carcinoma in situ (CIS) already involves ‘reactive’ tumour fibrosis(原位癌时期就已经存在活化的fibroblast)。it might provide protection from invasive or
    malignant conversion of CIS. Generally, the action of the cancer stroma is viewed as promoting cancer initiation and progression
  • IL-1β secretion by immune cells in early lesions emerges as a potential initiator of nuclear factor-κB (NF-κB) signalling in fibroblasts, instructing them to produce a pro-tumorigenic secretome
  • Reactive tumour stroma or fibrosis generally presents with an increased number of activated fibroblasts that usually express αSMA or fibroblast activation protein (FAP) and exhibit increased deposition of collagens, fibrin and other ECM constituents compared with quies cent fibroblasts. The recruitment of new capillaries and accumulation of immune cells within the TME are mediated by many different growth factors, such as VEGFA, PDGF, epidermal growth factor (EGF), IL-6 and IL-8, among others. In addition to cancer cell pro- duction of VEGFA, a source of host-derived VEGFA is activated fibroblasts.
  • Periostin and tenascin C, produced in part by activated fibroblasts, are generally absent in normal adult mammary tissue, but they become expressed in breast cancer

Fibroblast function and heterogeneity

  • It is now clear that many different markers can identify activated fibroblasts. Such markers include fibroblast- specific protein 1 (FSP1; also known as S100A4), vimentin, αSMA, FAP, PDGF receptor-α (PDGFRα), PDGFRβ, desmin and discoidin domain-containing receptor 2 (DDR2).Of note, PDGF receptors (PDGFRα and PDGFRβ) and the associated downstream signalling can be pharmacologically targeted . FSP1 is a reliable marker to detect quiescent, non-proliferating (Ki67−) fibroblasts

  • 但是这些marker都不是特异的，比如FSP1 also identifies macrophages and possibly other immune cells and is expressed by some cancer cells. FAP is also present in a subset of CD45+
    immune cells. Desmin and PDGFRβ are also expressed in perivascular cells

  • Loss of caveolin 1 (CAV1) expression in breast tumour CAFs may define metabolically altered fibroblasts with pro-tumorigenic functions, yet high expression of CAV1 in CAFs could also facilitate tumour invasion via ECM remodelling（乳腺癌中CAV1减少可促进癌症，但是增加会通过ECM重塑肿瘤的侵袭性）

  • The heterogeneity of fibroblasts, in particular those that are activated from their quiescent state, could also depend on the origin of the precursor fibroblasts. Activated fibroblasts are reported to arise from bone marrow-derived precursors, MSCs, endothelial cells, liver and pancreas stellate cells, resting tissue fibroblast and possibly from some types of epithelial cell(Fibroblast 的异质性与它的起源有关，相应的功能也可能有很大区别)

    Epigenetic regulation of fibroblasts

    • The signals that mediate the transition of resting fibroblasts into CAFs in tumours are probably complex, but in culture some of the phenotypic features associated with CAFs can be induced by TGFβ1. Nevertheless, emerging data suggest that the irreversible activation of fibroblasts might be driven by epigenetic alterations.(沉默Fibroblast向CAF转化的过程可能是由表观遗传调控的)

    • Studies with human breast tumours show that stromal cells (presumably fibroblasts) have unique epigenetic changes that are not observed in fibroblasts from normal mammary tissue

    • When FAFs are treated with the demethylating agent 5-azacytidine, the proliferation rate is reduced and collagen I synthesis and αSMA expression is decreased. Hypermethylation of RAS protein activator like 1 (RASAL1) and down regulation of RASAL1 protein lead to de novo activation of RAS in kidney FAFs but not in fibroblasts isolated from normal tissue or fibroblasts associated with acute kidney injury(使用去甲基化药物后，肿瘤中胶原减少，αSMA减少。RASAL1的过甲基化激活肾脏FAF中的RAS)
    • Prostate stromal cells with over-expression of the epigenetic regulator high-mobility group AT-hook 2 (HMGA2) were sufficient to induce prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia
    • An epigenetic switch implicating the regulation of leukaemia inhibitory factor (LIF) may result in the sustained and proinvasive functions of CAFs, via enhanced Janus kinase 1 ( JAK1)–signal transducer and activator of transcription 3 ( STAT3) activation

    CAFs: positive regulators of cancer

    CAFs in tumorigenesis

    The ability of CAFs to influence tumour growth was partly dependent on their ability to induce angiogenesis by CAF- derived stromal cell-derived factor 1 (SDF1; also known as CXCL12)
