Posted on 2020-09-28 | In paper

Dissecting the Single-Cell Transcriptome Network Underlying Gastric Premalignant Lesions and Early Gastric Cancer

Abstract

  • constructed a single-cell atlas for 32,332 high-quality cells from gastric antral mucosa biopsies of patients spanning a cascade of gastric premalignant lesions and early gastric cancer (EGC) using single-cell RNA sequencing

  • We found that gland mucous cells tended to acquire an intestinal-like stem cell phenotype during metaplasia, and we identified OR51E1 as a marker for unique endocrine cells in the early-malignant lesion.

  • We also found that HES6 might mark the pre-goblet cell cluster, potentially aiding identification of metaplasia at the early stage.

RESULTS

A Single-Cell Atlas of Premalignant Gastric Antral Mucosae and EGC

  • the atlas mainly comprised epithelial cells (EPCAM, KRT18, and MUC1) and non-epithelial cells (VIM and PTPRC)
  • epithelial cells mainly consisted of gastric epithelial cells, including antral basal gland mucous cells (GMCs, marked as MUC6 and TFF2), pit mucous cells (PMCs, marked as MUC5AC and TFF1), chief cells (PGA4 and PGA3), and enteroendocrine cells (CHGA and CHGB) GMC：基底腺粘液细胞 PMC：坑粘液细胞 chief cell ：主细胞 肠内分泌细胞
  • proliferative cells (PCs) MKI67 and BRIC5
  • cancer cells CEACAM5 and CEACAM6
  • metaplastic stem-like cell (MSC)：elevated expression of the intestinal stem cell markers, including OLFM4, EPHB2, and SOX9
  • non-epithelial cell lineages comprised T cells (CD2 and CD3D), B cells (CD79A), macrophages (CSF1R and CD68), fibroblasts (DCN and PDPN), smooth muscle cells (SMCs, marked as ACTA2), endothelial cells (ECs, marked as VWF and ENG), and mast cells (TPSAB1)
  • FigD: plot the cell of origin for some mediators in the cytokines and the nuclear factor kB (NF-kB) signaling pathway

we found that macrophages might be a dominant source of interleukin-1B (IL-1B), whose genotype was reported to confer a greater risk of GC, and PTGS2, which plays a crucial role in mediating the inflammatory process through the activation of NF-kB
